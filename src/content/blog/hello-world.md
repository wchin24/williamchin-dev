Every developer I admire writes. Not because they have to — but because explaining things forces clarity that code alone can't provide.

This is my attempt at that.

## What I'll Write About

I build iOS apps end-to-end: from the initial idea and PRD, through architecture decisions, Swift implementation, and App Store submission. Along the way there are a hundred small decisions that feel obvious in hindsight but aren't at all in the moment.

That's what I want to capture here — the reasoning behind decisions, not just the outcomes.

**A few recurring topics:**

- **iOS architecture** — MVVM, SwiftUI, Swift 6 concurrency, and when to break the rules
- **Product thinking as a solo developer** — how PM skills change when you're also the engineer
- **The indie developer experience** — what it actually takes to ship something polished by yourself
- **Tools and workflow** — how I use AI coding assistants, Xcode, and other tooling effectively

## Why Now

I've been building apps professionally for a few years, and recently went deeper on the indie side — building apps I want to exist, on my own terms.

Starting to write felt like the right next step. A forcing function to reflect on what I'm learning, and hopefully useful to someone else on a similar path.
